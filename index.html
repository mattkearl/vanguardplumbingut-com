Common Questions

Everything you'd want to know before calling — answered straight.

Is Vanguard Plumbing licensed and insured in Utah?

Yes. Vanguard Plumbing is fully licensed by the State of Utah and carries full general liability insurance. Derek Wallace meets all Utah Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing (DOPL) requirements for plumbing contractors. License information is available upon request.

What cities do you serve in Southern Utah?

We serve all of Washington County, Utah — St. George, Washington, Santa Clara, Ivins, Hurricane, LaVerkin, Toquerville, Leeds, Springdale, Apple Valley, Hildale, and Enterprise. We also cover Cedar City to the north and Mesquite, Nevada to the south. Not sure if we reach your area? Call and we'll tell you.

How fast can you respond to a plumbing emergency?

For emergencies in St. George, Washington, and the surrounding area we typically respond within 30–60 minutes. Derek answers calls 24 hours a day, 7 days a week including holidays — no voicemail, no dispatch. Call (435) 705-1295 any time.

Do you offer free estimates?

Yes. Free estimates on all non-emergency plumbing jobs throughout Southern Utah. Call or submit a request online and Derek will get back to you within a few hours with honest, straight pricing — no surprise charges.

What plumbing services does Vanguard offer?

Water heater installation and repair (tank and tankless), drain cleaning, slab leak detection, pipe repair and replacement, bathroom remodel plumbing, kitchen plumbing, garbage disposals, water softener hookups, sewer and water line services, and new construction rough-ins throughout Southern Utah.

Do you work on new construction in Washington County?

Yes. Derek has extensive experience with rough-in plumbing for custom homes throughout Washington County. If you're a general contractor, builder, or developer in St. George, Washington, Santa Clara, or anywhere in Southern Utah, call to discuss your project and timeline.

I have a burst pipe — what do I do right now?

Shut off your main water supply first — the valve is usually near the water meter or where the main line enters the house. Then call (435) 705-1295 immediately. Derek answers 24/7. The sooner the water is off, the less damage you'll face.

Do you work on weekends?

Regular hours include Saturdays 8am–4pm. For after-hours emergencies — burst pipes, flooding, no hot water — call any time. Derek answers 24/7 and will be straight with you about what needs immediate attention and what can wait until morning.
