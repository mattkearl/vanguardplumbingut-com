# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Homeowners, property managers, and contractors in Washington, St. George, and the broader Southern Utah / Mesquite NV corridor. They're searching for a plumber they can trust — someone who won't cut corners, trash the job site, or ghost them. They range from first-time homeowners with a burst pipe to custom-home builders sourcing a reliable sub. The context is often mild stress: something broke, a project is starting, or a deadline is looming. They make their call fast.

## Product Purpose

Convert visitors into service requests — a phone call or a form submission — by proving Derek Wallace is the most trustworthy, skilled, and professional plumber in Southern Utah. The core competitive claim is "The Vanguard Standard": a 4-pillar brand promise of integrity, gallery-grade precision, zero-mess jobsites, and modern/tech-forward service. Every design decision should make that promise feel real, not like marketing copy.

## Brand Personality

**Trustworthy · Premium · Tough.** Old-school integrity meets high-craft work. Derek is a real guy with a real story — built on hard work, not a franchise playbook. The brand is elevated but never slick or impersonal. It should feel like a craftsman who takes pride in his work, not a corporate plumbing chain.

Voice: direct, confident, unpretentious. No jargon, no hype. Specifics over superlatives.

## Anti-references

- **Franchise/template plumber sites** (Mr. Rooter, Roto-Rooter aesthetic): stock wrench clip-art, orange-and-blue color combos, cheesy contractor stock photography, generic trust badges, "We're #1!" rhetoric.
- **Overly corporate / sterile**: big-box contractor feel — too polished, too impersonal, zero soul. Looks like a national roll-out, not a local craftsman.
- **Dark & grungy DIY vibes**: low-budget dark backgrounds, yellow-on-black, spray-paint energy, cluttered layouts.

## Design Principles

1. **The standard is the differentiator.** The Vanguard Standard (Oath / Nordic Precision / Spotless Shield / Next-Gen Service) is what sets Derek apart. Design should make those 4 pillars feel like a real, earned identity — not a slogan bolted on.
2. **Show, don't tell.** Real photos of Derek's actual work do more trust work than any badge or claim. Prioritize his images over stock; let the gallery prove the craftsmanship.
3. **Premium without pretense.** The design is elevated and clean, but never cold or corporate. Derek is a person, not a brand department. The "Meet Derek" section is load-bearing.
4. **Trust lives in the details.** A well-crafted website signals a well-crafted job. Tight typography, consistent spacing, no broken images, no lorem ipsum, no approximations.
5. **Proximity converts.** Service areas, phone number, and form must be effortless to reach from anywhere on the page. Reduce friction to contact at every turn.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. The user base spans ages — from young homeowners to older contractors — so body text contrast must be solid (≥4.5:1), tap targets sized generously, and forms fully keyboard-navigable. No motion that can't be suppressed via `prefers-reduced-motion`.
